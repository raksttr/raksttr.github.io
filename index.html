Poverty is a pervasive widespread condition that acts as a profound barrier to human flourishing, it viciously leaves its victims of people and families in multi-genrational economic, social, and educational disadvantedges. To overcome poverty we need a new mindset, acquire new skills, and adopting strict financial discipline. Common Questions

What is poverty?

Poverty is the state of lacking the financial resources and essential items needed to maintain a basic standard of living, including food, clean water, shelter, and clothing. Statistically, children under age 5, female-headed households, and racial minorities—specifically Native American, Black, and Hispanic communities—are affected the most by poverty in the U.S.. Children have the highest poverty rate of any age group, while Southern regions experience the highest rates of poverty.
